Welcome to Bentota Turtle Hatchery

Here at the Bentota Turtle Hatchery, we are dedicated to protecting and conserving sea turtle populations. Our hatchery is home to several species of sea turtles, and we work tirelessly to ensure their survival. Come visit us and learn more about these amazing creatures!

Bentota Turtle Hatcheries is a Sri Lankan conservation project dedicated to the protection of sea turtles and their eggs. The project was established in response to the loss of turtle populations as a result of overfishing, habitat damage, and poaching. The hatcheries are situated along the shore of Bentota, a major tourist attraction on Sri Lanka's southwestern coast.

The hatcheries' main purpose is to collect sea turtle eggs from vulnerable nests along the shore and transport them to a safe location within the hatchery. The eggs are then incubated until they hatch, at which point the newborn turtles are returned to the ocean. The hatcheries want to raise the survival rate of sea turtle hatchlings and expand their overall population by doing so.

What We Do

Our hatchery offers a variety of activities, including:

  • Turtle feeding
  • Egg hatching demonstrations
  • Nesting site visits
  • Turtle releases

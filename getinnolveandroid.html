About Turtle Conservation in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka is home to five species of sea turtles, all of which are endangered due to habitat destruction, pollution, and hunting. Turtle conservation projects in Sri Lanka work to protect nesting sites, monitor turtle populations, and raise awareness about the importance of sea turtle conservation.

Volunteering with Turtle Conservation Projects in Sri Lanka

There are many organizations and projects throughout Sri Lanka that focus on sea turtle conservation. These projects offer a variety of opportunities for volunteers to get involved, including:

  • Monitoring and recording turtle nesting activity
  • Assisting with beach patrols to protect turtle nests from predators and poachers
  • Participating in hatchling releases
  • Helping with the care and rehabilitation of injured turtles
  • Conducting research on turtle behavior and ecology

By volunteering with a sea turtle conservation project in Sri Lanka, you can help protect these incredible creatures and ensure their survival for generations to come.

Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka is a beautiful island country located in South Asia. It is known for its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and diverse wildlife.

Sri Lanka is home to several species of sea turtles, including the green turtle, hawksbill turtle, loggerhead turtle, and leatherback turtle. These turtles play a crucial role in maintaining the health of the ocean ecosystem, and are an important part of Sri Lankan culture and history.

Unfortunately, sea turtles in Sri Lanka are facing numerous threats to their survival, including habitat loss, pollution, and overfishing. In addition, many turtles are accidentally caught in fishing nets or are hunted for their meat, eggs, and shells.

That's why conservation efforts are so important in Sri Lanka. Turtle hatcheries and other conservation organizations work tirelessly to protect the turtles and their eggs, and to raise awareness about the importance of these magnificent creatures.

Sri Lankan Wildlife

Sri Lanka is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including elephants, leopards, sloth bears, and many species of birds. In addition, Sri Lanka is also known for its sea turtles.

Asian elephants

Sri Lanka has the highest density of elephants in Asia, with an estimated population of around 7,500. These majestic creatures can be found in several national parks, such as Udawalawe, Yala, and Minneriya.

Leopards

Sri Lanka has the highest density of leopards in the world, with an estimated population of around 1,000. These elusive big cats can be found in Yala National Park, Wilpattu National Park, and several other protected areas.

Sloth bears

These shaggy-coated bears are found in the dry zone of Sri Lanka, and can be spotted in national parks like Yala and Wilpattu.

Birds

Sri Lanka is a birdwatcher's paradise, with over 400 species of birds, including several endemic species like the Sri Lankan junglefowl, Sri Lankan hanging parrot, and Sri Lanka blue magpie. Popular birdwatching sites include Sinharaja Forest Reserve, Horton Plains National Park, and Bundala National Park.

Sea turtles

Green turtles, hawksbill turtles, loggerhead turtles, and leatherback turtles are all found in Sri Lanka.
